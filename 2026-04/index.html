Site Infrastructure — Q1 Wins
Three infrastructure improvements behind the traffic growth.
  1. Removed Elementor dependency entirely. Pages now ship clean, faster HTML — no page-builder overhead, smaller JS payload, cleaner DOM for crawlers.
  2. Server response time dropped from 400–600 ms to under 200 ms (since 18 March). Faster response = more pages Googlebot crawls per session = more of the site indexed, faster.
  3. Average keyword ranking is the highest in 16 months and still climbing. 16-month trajectory: position 45 → 7.2 on a volume-weighted basis — driven by the SEO work shipped this quarter.
Crawl Response Time
GSC Crawl Stats — dropped sharply after 18 March.
Before: 400–600 ms average response on every Googlebot request. High variance. The Jan–mid-March line is jagged.
After (since 18 March): Consistently under 200 ms. Latest reading: ~150 ms. Google can now crawl roughly 2.5× more pages in the same crawl budget.

What We're Doing in April & May
Four priorities, ordered by expected business impact.
  1. Launch 58 feature pages in 40 days. One page per day under /features/. Every card on the features hub gets its own dedicated page — targeted commercial keywords per page (job posting, recruitment dashboard, candidate screening, AI interview, candidate matching, etc.).
  2. Integrated Google Ads + SEO strategy. Audit every paid-bidding keyword and map it against organic coverage. Where we already rank, pause or reduce paid. Where we don't, use paid to reach the top while organic catches up. Single spreadsheet feeds both teams.
  3. Commercial keyword push to page 1. "AI recruitment software" (17), "AI hiring software" (14), "AI recruiter" (5) — all within striking distance. On-page optimization + internal links from the 58 new feature pages. Target: one into top 3 by end of May.
  4. Enable CDN for page speed. Builds on the 200 ms response-time win. CDN adds edge caching across geographies — further improves Core Web Vitals and international search performance.
  5. Fix attribution (HubSpot → Freshsales UTM gap). Deploy UTM capture on the 15 lead-capture forms, map to cf_traffic_source in Freshsales. May report then includes organic-attributed contact and deal volume for the first time.
What changes in the May report. UTM capture deployed = organic-attributed leads visible. 20–25 new feature pages live = first impressions and clicks landing. CDN = Core Web Vitals improved. One commercial keyword should hit top 3.
